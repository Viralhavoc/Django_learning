Hedgehogs

Small, nocturnal mammals known for their spiny coats and solitary habits.

Beginner's Guide

Overview

Hedgehogs are small mammals covered with spines (modified hairs). Native to parts of Europe, Asia and Africa, they have been kept as pets in many parts of the world. Hedgehogs are primarily nocturnal and are known for rolling into a ball when threatened.

Quick Facts

Diet
Insects, worms, some fruits, and occasionally small vertebrates.
Size
Typically 5–12 inches (varies by species).
Behavior
Solitary, mostly nocturnal, and uses scent for navigation.
Defense
Rolls into a spiny ball; quills do not detach like porcupines'.

Responsible Care Tips

  • Consult a vet experienced with exotic pets before adopting.
  • Provide a spacious enclosure, hiding places, and a consistent temperature.
  • Feed a balanced diet; avoid raw milk and sugary foods.
  • Handle gently and infrequently at first to build trust.

Learn More

If you're considering a hedgehog as a pet, research local regulations and speak to breeders or rescue organizations. Wild hedgehogs should not be kept as pets — if you find an injured wild hedgehog, contact a wildlife rehabilitator.

Habitat & Range

Hedgehogs inhabit woodlands, grasslands, and suburban gardens. Their range varies by species.

Common Questions

Are hedgehogs good pets?
They can be rewarding but require specific care and may be illegal in some areas.
Do they hibernate?
Some species undergo torpor or hibernation depending on climate; captive hedgehogs often need controlled temperatures.
